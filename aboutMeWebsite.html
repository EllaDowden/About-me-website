Learn a bit about me:

I am a 23 year old, recent Psychology graduate from London. The main focus of my degree were developmental and clinical psychology. I love working with children and understanding how their brains and behaviours develop! I wrote my final dissertation on Reading comprehension in children with visual impairments.

I also have a neurological sleep condition called Narcolepsy. This is caused by a deciciency of the main chemical that controls the sleep/wake cycle in the brain. The main symptoms include excessive daytime fatigue (similar to a neurotypical person being sleep depreived for upto 48 hours!) and cataplexy. This has had a significant impact on my life over the past decade and is a large part of why I began my coding journey. I believe that a career in tech may be more accessible for my disability due to the freedom to work remotely!

I began my coding journey this autumn as a way of passing the time on medical leave. But I quickly discovered how much I enjoyed learning this new skill. Being able to express my creativity in a new way has been both cathartic and exciting for me so I sought out to find an apprentiship that could help me learn even more! Luckily I stumbled across Coders and Founders on tiktok. My expereience attending their pre-appication coding meetings was better then I could've hoped for and solidified my goal to apply for their skills bootcamp! The heplful and friendly atmosphere really encouraged me to keep working towards that goal.

  • Taylow Swift! I've been obsessed with her since I was about 10 years old
  • Coding. I began my coding journey this year!

I am Applying for Foudners and Coders for a number of different reasons. I've spent the past year on medical leave due to my condition and decided to take up coding as a new hobby this summer. I had perviously never thought I could become good enough to utilize it in my career.
